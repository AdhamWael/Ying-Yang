Part 04

‚ö†Ô∏è Challenges We Overcame

The obstacles we encountered and the innovative solutions we developed

01

Parametric Precision

‚ö†Ô∏è The Challenge:

Finding the perfect sine function amplitude and period to create a smooth, natural-looking S-curve that divides the circle exactly in half while maintaining the yin-yang aesthetic.

‚úì Our Solution:

Tested multiple coefficient combinations and settled on x = 3sin(y/4) for optimal balance. The coefficient 3 provides the perfect horizontal displacement, while y/4 ensures the curve completes exactly one cycle across the pool's Radius.

02

Boundary Conditions

‚ö†Ô∏è The Challenge:

Ensuring the parametric equations met the boundary conditions of the circular pool, particularly at the points where the curves intersect the circle.

‚úì Our Solution:

With hard work and perseverance, we went in every detail to make sure it meets the boundary conditions of the circular pool, particularly at the points where the curves intersect the circle.

03

3D Translation

‚ö†Ô∏è The Challenge:

Converting 2D mathematical equations into a realistic 3D pool model with proper depth, maintaining the precise curves while adding a third dimension.

‚úì Our Solution:

Used Blender's parametric surface tools to extrude our 2D curves into 3D geometry while preserving mathematical accuracy.

04

Physical Model Creation

‚ö†Ô∏è The Challenge:

Creating a physical prototype that accurately represents our mathematical design while being practical to fabricate at small scale.

‚úì Our Solution:

Crafted a 13.5cm model using clay, carefully shaping it to match our parametric equations. This hands-on approach allowed us to verify the design's physical feasibility and aesthetic appeal.

05

Practical Scaling

‚ö†Ô∏è The Challenge:

Scaling from our 13.5cm clay model to a real 13.5m pool while maintaining all proportions, curves, and mathematical relationships intact.

‚úì Our Solution:

Established a precise 1:100 scale ratio. Used this ratio to scale all dimensions proportionally, ensuring that the physical model maintained the same proportions as the original design with 13.5cm Radius and 8.5cm height.
